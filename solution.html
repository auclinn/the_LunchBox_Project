HOW TO REDUCE FOOD WASTE?

Various initiatives and solutions are being implemented to combat food waste in the Philippines, addressing the critical issue of wastage while millions go hungry. These efforts encompass a range of strategies aimed at reducing food waste at different levels of society, from individual households to legislative measures:

Initiatives and Solutions in Food Waste in the Philippines



Promoting the Purchase of "Ugly Produce"

Encouraging consumers to buy imperfect or oddly-shaped produce helps prevent these items from being discarded due to cosmetic imperfections. This initiative aims to reduce food waste at the retail level and promote the consumption of nutritious but visually imperfect fruits and vegetables.

Meal Planning and FIFO Practices

Advocating for meal planning and the FIFO (first in, first out) method in households and businesses helps prevent food spoilage and ensures that older items are used before newer ones. These practices promote efficient food consumption and reduce unnecessary waste.

Cooking Leftovers

Transforming leftovers into new dishes is a practical way to minimize food waste. By creatively repurposing excess food, households can reduce the amount of edible food that ends up in the trash, contributing to a more sustainable approach to food consumption.

Zero Food Waste Act of 2022

The proposed Zero Food Waste Act of 2022 aims to establish a system that promotes food waste reduction through redistribution and recycling efforts. This legislative measure seeks to raise awareness, implement strategies for waste reduction, and engage specific government agencies in a national campaign to combat food waste.

Sharing Excess Food

Encouraging the sharing of excess food within communities helps prevent surplus items from going to waste. By redistributing food that would otherwise be discarded, individuals and organizations can support those in need and reduce overall food waste.

Composting & Recycling

Initiatives like composting and recycling help reduce food waste by converting organic waste into nutrient-rich soil and reducing the amount of waste sent to landfills. These practices also promote sustainable agriculture and reduce the environmental impact of food production and consumption.

Global Strategies:

Policy and Regulation:

Governments can implement policies that incentivize food waste reduction, such as tax breaks for food donations and penalties for excessive waste. National food waste reduction targets and comprehensive waste management policies can also drive significant change. For instance, France has banned supermarkets from throwing away unsold food, requiring them to donate it to charities instead.

International Collaboration:

Global partnerships and agreements can facilitate the sharing of best practices and technologies for reducing food waste. The United Nations' Sustainable Development Goal 12.3 aims to halve per capita global food waste at the retail and consumer levels by 2030. Collaborative efforts such as the Champions 12.3 coalition bring together leaders from government, business, and civil society to inspire and monitor progress.

Sustainable Supply Chains:

Encouraging sustainable practices throughout the supply chain, from production to consumption, can minimize waste and promote efficiency. This includes better crop planning, improved storage and transportation infrastructure, and innovations in food processing and packaging. Techniques such as precision agriculture, cold chain logistics, and smart packaging can significantly reduce food loss and waste.







Food Recovery Hierarchy

The Food Recovery Hierarchy by the EPA is a prioritized framework that outlines actions organizations can take to prevent and divert wasted food effectively. The hierarchy is structured in tiers, with each level focusing on different management strategies for dealing with wasted food. The top tiers of the hierarchy are considered the best ways to prevent and divert food waste as they offer the most benefits for the environment, society, and the economy.


Source Reduction

This tier focuses on preventing food waste in the first place by reducing the amount of surplus food generated throughout the food supply chain. Strategies include accurate forecasting, improved inventory management, and optimizing food production and distribution to match demand.

Feed Hungry People

This tier emphasizes donating excess, edible food to food banks, soup kitchens, and other charitable organizations to feed people in need. Effective food donation programs can help reduce food insecurity and divert usable food from landfills.

Feed Animals

If food is not suitable for human consumption, it can be diverted to feed animals, such as livestock or pets. This option helps reduce the amount of food waste while providing nutritional value to animals.

Industrial Uses

Food waste that cannot be used for human or animal consumption can be repurposed for industrial uses, such as converting it into biofuel, biogas, or other industrial products.

Composting

When food waste cannot be prevented or diverted for other uses, composting is a preferred method to convert the organic matter into a nutrient-rich soil amendment, which can be used to enrich soils and support plant growth.

Landfill/Incineration

The least preferred option is disposing of food waste in landfills or through incineration, as these methods have the highest environmental impact and do not contribute to the circular economy.
